HOME CREDIT DEFAULT RISK DATASET ANALYSIS¶

PROBLEM STATEMENT AND OBJECTIVE¶

Problem Statement¶

Financial institutions face substantial losses when borrowers default on loans. Accurately identifying high-risk applicants before loan approval is crucial for minimizing financial losses and improving lending decisions. Traditional evaluation metrics alone may not adequately reflect the business consequences of incorrect classifications.

Objective¶

The objectives of this project are to:

  • Predict loan default risk using machine learning.
  • Clean and preprocess credit risk data.
  • Compare Logistic Regression and CatBoost models.
  • Evaluate model performance using classification metrics.
  • Define business costs for misclassifications.
  • Optimize the classification threshold to minimize total business cost.
  • Generate actionable recommendations for credit risk management.

DATASET DESCRIPTION¶

Dataset Name¶

Credit Risk Dataset

Dataset Overview¶

The Credit Risk Dataset contains information about loan applicants, including demographic characteristics, financial history, employment status, loan details, and credit behavior.

The primary objective of the dataset is to predict whether a borrower is likely to default on a loan. This enables financial institutions to make informed lending decisions, reduce financial losses, and improve risk management strategies.

The target variable is loan_status, which indicates whether a borrower defaulted on a loan.

Features Description¶

To choose a solver, you might want to consider the following aspects:

- 'lbfgs' is a good default solver because it works reasonably well for a wide

BUSINESS COST ANALYSIS¶

In [34]:
# Cost Definition 
FP_COST = 1000
FN_COST = 10000

Justification¶

  • False Positive = Good borrower rejected
  • False Negative = Defaulter approved

False negatives are significantly more expensive.

In [35]:
# Threshold Optimization 
thresholds = np.arange(0.1,
                       1.0,
                       0.01)

costs = []

for threshold in thresholds:

    pred = (cat_prob >= threshold).astype(int)

    tn, fp, fn, tp = confusion_matrix(y_test,
                                      pred).ravel()

    cost = (fp * FP_COST) + (fn * FN_COST)

    costs.append(cost)
In [36]:
# Best Threshold 
best_threshold = thresholds[np.argmin(costs)]

      best_threshold)

BUSINESS INSIGHTS AND RECOMMENDATIONS¶

Key Insights¶

A. Loan Default Distribution¶

  • The dataset is moderately imbalanced, with significantly more non-default loans than default loans.
  • Most borrowers successfully repay their loans, while a smaller proportion represent high-risk customers.
  • This imbalance highlights the importance of using metrics beyond accuracy when evaluating model performance.

B. Borrower Characteristics¶

1. Income Distribution¶

  • Most borrowers fall within lower to moderate income brackets.
  • A small number of borrowers have exceptionally high incomes, creating a right-skewed distribution.
  • Income alone does not fully explain default behavior, suggesting the need to consider multiple risk factors.

2. Loan Intent Analysis¶

  • Debt consolidation and personal loans appear among the most common loan purposes.
  • Different loan purposes exhibit varying levels of default risk, indicating that loan intent contributes to credit risk assessment.

3. Previous Default History¶

  • Customers with previous defaults show a higher likelihood of future default.
  • Historical credit behavior remains one of the strongest indicators of future repayment performance.

C. Model Performance Insights¶

1. Logistic Regression¶

  • Produced strong classification performance and serves as a highly interpretable baseline model.
  • Successfully identified many default cases while maintaining reasonable precision.

2. CatBoost¶

  • Outperformed Logistic Regression across most evaluation metrics.
  • Demonstrated stronger ability to capture complex relationships between borrower attributes and default behavior.
  • Generated a higher ROC-AUC score, indicating superior discrimination between default and non-default borrowers.

D. ROC Curve Analysis¶

  • Both models performed significantly better than random classification.
  • CatBoost maintained higher true positive rates across various thresholds. This suggests better identification of risky borrowers without excessively increasing false positives.

E. Business Cost Analysis¶

The threshold optimization plot reveals an important business finding:

  • Default classification thresholds have a direct impact on organizational cost.
  • Very low thresholds increase false positives, leading to unnecessary loan rejections.
  • Very high thresholds increase false negatives, allowing high-risk borrowers to receive loans.
  • An optimal threshold exists where total business cost is minimized. This demonstrates that the best business decision is not always achieved using the default threshold of 0.50.

Recommendations¶

  1. Risk-Based Lending Strategy

    • Implement CatBoost as the primary credit risk model due to its stronger predictive performance.
    • Use risk scores to categorize applicants into low, medium, and high-risk groups.

  2. Threshold Optimization

    • Adopt the optimized threshold identified through business cost analysis rather than relying on the default classification threshold.
    • Continuously monitor threshold performance as borrower behavior evolves.

  3. Strengthen Credit Assessment

    Place greater emphasis on:

    • Previous default history
    • Credit history length
    • Loan-to-income ratio
    • Loan grade

  4. Portfolio Risk Management

    • Apply enhanced review processes for high-risk borrowers.
    • Introduce risk-adjusted interest rates based on predicted default probabilities.

  5. Model Monitoring

    • Retrain models periodically using updated borrower information.
    • Monitor performance drift to ensure sustained predictive accuracy.

CONCLUSION¶

This project successfully developed machine learning models for credit risk prediction and business cost optimization. Following preprocessing, exploratory analysis, and feature engineering, Logistic Regression and CatBoost models were trained and evaluated.

CatBoost demonstrated superior predictive performance and achieved stronger discriminatory power than Logistic Regression. Business cost analysis further revealed that optimizing the classification threshold significantly reduces expected financial losses associated with poor lending decisions.

The results show that machine learning can enhance credit risk management by improving default prediction accuracy, supporting better lending decisions, and minimizing financial risk exposure.
